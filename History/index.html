A Journey Through the culture of Turkey

Turkey is country with long history and empires dating back 4000 years ago. The course of time and events introduced Turkey to a wide range of people and cultures. Therefore the country has a rich culture of unique traditions and customs. It is located in both Europe and Asia which enforeces the rich culture even more.

History

Turkey has a long history of predecessors Islamic Empires in middle east such as the Byzantian empire, the Seljuk empire and lastly the Ottoman Empire. After World war I the Ottoman empire collapsed, and large parts of Turkey today were occupied by the Allied forces and some other countries. Then Mustafa Kemal Pasha, a high respected and high ranking general, started the Turkish nationalist resistance against the Ottoman government and the Allied powers. This led to the Turkish War of Independence. Mustafa Kemal Pasha won the war and established the Republic of Turkey. Mustafa Kemal Pasha became the founding father of the Republic of Turkey and after the surname revolution, one of Ataturk’s revolutions, he took the surname Ataturk, which means “Father of the Turks”. Ataturk revolutionized Turkey and established modern standards in Turkey. Turkey is still the only Islamic country in which state and religion are separated. The country of the Republic of Turkey basically serves as a bridge between Asia and Europe that is why it is influenced by both cultures.

People in Turkey

Turkey's population is very diverse with various ethnic, cultural and religious backgrounds. Turks are the largest ethnic group in turkey and they are known for their hospitality and their respect for their elders. The country is predominatly a muslim country. Family is very important in turkish culture and respect for elder is essential. Hospitality is also an essential part of turkish culture and therefore it is normal to offer guests food or drinks. Turkish people are also known for their love for cats and dogs. Many locals in big cities look after stray cats and dogs. This love is reflected in Turkey's history in the Ottoman Empire, which was known for loving animals.
